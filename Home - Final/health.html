Why did WHO choose Greek alphabets as names for COVID-19 variants?

WHO chose Greek alphabets as COVID-19 variants' names after months of deliberations on possibilities such as names of Greek Gods and pseudo-classical names, bacteriologist Mark Pallen said. Idea to refer variants of concern as VOC1, VOC2 was scrapped after he pointed out it resembled English swear words. WHO stated the variants have been named to simplify pronunciation and avoid stigma.

Diabetes care falls short for most of the world's population

The International Diabetes Federation notes that of the estimated 463 million adults in the world who have diabetes, almost 80% live in low and middle income countries.“Diabetes continues to explode everywhere, in every country, and 80% of people with it live in these low and middle income countries,” says lead author David Flood, M.D., M.Sc., who is a national clinician scholar at the University of Michigan Institute for Healthcare Policy & Innovation.

We'll be able to vaccinate 1 cr people daily by July or August: Govt

The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) on Tuesday said there is no shortage of vaccine and they are confident of vaccinating the whole population by December. "By mid-July or August, we will have enough doses to vaccinate one crore people daily," ICMR Director General Balram Bhargava said. Over 21 crore vaccine doses have been administered in India so far.

How has India's COVID-19 positivity rate changed in the month of May?

In the week beginning on April 27 and ending on May 3, India's COVID-19 positivity rate was 21.29% and in the week ending on May 10, the positivity rate was 21.38%. There has been a steady decline in weekly positivity rate since last three weeks, shows official data. It has been declining even when testing has increased multifold, said government.

Provide details of PM CARES Fund for orphaned kids: SC to Centre

The Supreme Court on Tuesday sought details of the Centre's plan for providing relief to orphaned kids who lost their parents to COVID-19. It also asked for information on the monitoring of the scheme for which money would be provided from PM CARES Fund. The Centre announced to give orphaned children a corpus of ₹10 lakh once they turn 23.

Flyers arriving in Mumbai from other cities in Maha don't need COVID-19 test: BMC

The BMC has said that all domestic air passengers "originating from airports located within Maharashtra to Mumbai" are exempted from RT-PCR test requirement upon landing in Mumbai. However, air passengers arriving into Mumbai from outside Maharashtra have to compulsorily present a negative RT-PCR test report at the time of boarding the flight, the Mumbai airport authorities have said.

'Saudagar' art director Marutirao Kale dies of COVID-19 complications

Veteran art director Marutirao Kale, who worked in films like 'Saudagar' and 'Disco Dancer', has passed away due to COVID-19 complications in Mumbai. Kale, who was 92 years old, breathed his last on May 26, his daughter Meena Kapadia said. He had tested positive for COVID-19 on May 7 and was undergoing treatment at a hospital in Bandra, Meena added.

Former MP Minister Laxmikant Sharma dies due to COVID-19 aged 60

Senior BJP leader and former Madhya Pradesh Education Minister Laxmikant Sharma succumbed to COVID-19 at the age of 60 on Monday. After testing positive for the disease on May 11, Sharma was admitted to a private hospital in Bhopal where he died due to bilateral COVID-19 pneumonia, doctors said. He is survived by his wife and three daughters.

BMC polls to be held in Jan-Feb if COVID-19 under control: Mayor

Mumbai Mayor Kishori Pednekar on Tuesday said the elections to the Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation (BMC) will be held in January-February in 2022 if the COVID-19 situation is under control. The polls will be postponed if the cases don't decline, she added. On Monday, Maharashtra reported 15,077 new COVID-19 cases, which is the lowest in the last three months.

China administered over 66 cr doses of COVID-19 vaccines so far

More than 66.1 crore doses of COVID-19 vaccines have been administered in China as of May 31, according to the National Health Commission data on Tuesday. About 2.23 crore doses of COVID-19 vaccines were administered in the country on Monday, the data showed. COVID-19 has infected over 1.11 lakh people in China, according to the World Health Organization (WHO).
